# Presentation Rubric Self-Check

Book reference: Your Senior, p. 15

Use this before Presentation Day.

## Project Overview

- Exceeds (A Grade): student described the topic of the project and explained what they did or made in a clear and relevant manner.
- Meets (B): student described the topic.
- Does not meet (needs to present again): it is not clear what this project was all about.

## Connection To Program

- Exceeds (A Grade): it is clear what program the student is in and how the project connects to the program.
- Meets (B): the audience knows the student's program and sees a connection to the program area of study.
- Does not meet (needs to present again): the audience has no understanding of the project and no idea how it connects to an East Tech program.

My evidence:


## Explanation Of Final Process Or Product

- Exceeds (A Grade): the audience understood what the student went through and what the outcome of their hard work was.
- Meets (B): the audience understood the process the student went through to complete the project.
- Does not meet (needs to present again): the audience does not really know or understand what the student did.

## Presentation Skills

- Exceeds (A Grade): student was on time, organized, and rehearsed. The presentation was enjoyable for the audience.
- Meets (B): student was on time and organized.
- Does not meet (needs to present again): student was late, the presentation lacked organization, or the student clearly had not prepared.
